Canada Financial Guide 2025

Published: March 19, 2025 | 17 min read

Canada's financial system is known for its stability and strong regulatory framework. This guide covers banking, credit, mortgages, and investing for Canadian residents in 2025.

Canadian Banking Landscape

Canada's "Big Five" banks dominate: RBC, TD, Scotiabank, BMO, and CIBC. These institutions offer comprehensive services from basic chequing accounts to complex investment products.

Account Types

  • Chequing Accounts: Daily transactions, typically with monthly fees
  • Savings Accounts: Higher interest for stored funds
  • High-Interest Savings: Online banks offer 4-5% interest
  • US Dollar Accounts: For cross-border banking

Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)

The TFSA is Canada's flagship tax-advantaged account. For 2025, the contribution limit is $7,000, with a cumulative limit of $102,000 for those who were 18+ in 2009.

TFSA Benefits

  • Tax-free growth on investments
  • Tax-free withdrawals anytime
  • Withdrawals add back to contribution room next year
  • Can hold cash, stocks, bonds, ETFs, and more

Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)

RRSPs provide tax-deferred growth and immediate tax deductions. The 2025 contribution limit is 18% of earned income up to $32,490.

Canadian Mortgages

Key differences from other countries:

  • 5-Year Terms Common: Unlike 30-year US mortgages
  • Mortgage Insurance: Required for down payments under 20%
  • Stress Test: Must qualify at higher rate (5.25% or contract +2%)
  • Fixed vs Variable: Both popular options

Credit in Canada

Canadian credit scores range from 300-900. Two bureaus operate: Equifax and TransUnion. Building credit requires:

  • Consistent payment history
  • Low credit utilization
  • Mix of credit types
  • Length of credit history

Conclusion

Canada's financial system offers excellent tools for wealth building, particularly the TFSA and RRSP. Understanding these accounts and using them strategically can lead to significant tax savings and financial growth.
